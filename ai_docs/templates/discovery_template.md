# Discovery Document – Outline Template

## 1. Introduction & Thank You

### 1.1. Appreciation & Context
A brief thank-you acknowledging the client’s time during the discovery session. Reinforce that this document reflects your understanding of their goals, challenges, and working style.

### 1.2. Our Approach
Explain that your role is to tailor solutions to match the client’s current comfort level with technology and automation.  
The goal is to enhance what already works well in their business while introducing improvements that feel natural and manageable.

### 1.3. What to Expect in This Document
This document will provide:
- A clear description of how AI and automation can support their business  
- An explanation of where their current workflow sits in relation to modern tools  
- What becomes possible with one small step beyond their current level  
- A curated list of solution ideas, moving from simplest to most advanced  
- A practical set of recommended next steps  

---

## 2. Understanding Your Current Workflow & Technology Use

### 2.1. Current Position
Summarize the client’s present approach to tools, technology, and automation.  
Examples may include:
- Use of chat-based tools  
- Familiarity with certain platforms  
- Manual or semi-manual workflows  
- Current pain points  
- What is already working well  

(This section is customized per project.)

### 2.2. Opportunities in the Next Step Forward
Describe—in plain, non-technical language—what becomes possible by taking **one level of sophistication beyond** where they currently operate.  
This might include:
- Gentle automation  
- Simple recurring workflows  
- Light integration between existing tools  
- More proactive support instead of reactive processes  

This section bridges their current state to the upcoming recommendations **without referencing tiers or ladders.**

---

## 3. Core Tools & Basic Setup

Describe the foundational components needed to implement the recommendations.  
Keep this section high-level, flexible, and adaptable to any project.

### 3.1. Primary Data Source or System of Record
Identify the main location where business information currently lives (e.g., CRM, spreadsheet, inbox).  
Explain its role in enabling automations or improvements.

### 3.2. Automation or Integration Platform
Outline the tool(s) that will connect systems and streamline workflows.  
Examples: automation platforms, integration tools, or lightweight backend logic.

### 3.3. Communication Layer
Describe the channels through which the client will receive reminders, insights, or automated interactions.  
(e.g., SMS, email, voice, chat)

### 3.4. Optional Supporting Tools
Mention any additional integrations that may be relevant depending on scope or preference.

---

## 4. Potential Integration & Automation Ideas  
(From Easiest to Most Advanced)

A curated list of ideas based on the discovery conversation.  
This section is rewritten per client and should always reflect **only** what is appropriate for their current level and one step above.

Present ideas in ascending order of sophistication—for example:

### 4.1. Foundation-Level Improvements (Immediate & Low Effort)
Ideas that require minimal setup and offer quick wins.

### 4.2. Light Automations & Simple Workflows
Ideas that automate recurring tasks or reduce manual effort.

### 4.3. Enhanced Insights & Assisted Processes
Ideas that provide more context, summaries, or intelligence without requiring complex integrations.

### 4.4. Advanced Options (Still Within Reach)
Ideas that represent the “next step up,” offering more automation or deeper connections between systems—still aligned with their comfort level.

(Each subsection is filled with client-specific ideas drawn from your conversation.)

---

## 5. Recommended Next Steps

Provide a pragmatic, confidence-building set of next steps, such as:
- Suggested starting point  
- Tools or connections to set up first  
- Phased approach to implementation  
- What the initial MVP would include  
- How progress will be measured  

---

## 6. Closing & Invitation

A short, friendly closing paragraph reinforcing partnership, clarity, and support.  
Invite feedback and confirm the next step in the process.
